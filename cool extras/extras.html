Cool Extras!

In this section, I will discuss some interesting data that arises from the Spotify Dataset.

Similar Attributes:

The similarity of two attributes represents the amount of shared songs between the two attributes. I.e. two attributes are similar when a song having one attribute gives a high likelihood that the song will also have the other. The top three pairs of most similar attributes from the subset of songs from 1970-2030 includes:
  • very low loudness & very low energy
  • very low energy & very high acousticness
  • very low loudness & very high acoustcness
Unsurprisingly, a song having very low loudness is often associated with very low energy. The more interesting part is the link between high acousticness and low energy. Intuitively, acoustic music is calmer and is less harsh then usual music as by definition, it uses traditional instruments rather than electronic sound.

The 70s

The top attributes of the 70s are low loudness, low energy, and high acousticness. Although disco was one of the most established trends of the 70s, the decade was also characterized by new wave, pop, and soft rock which brought in acoustic and relative low energy sounds compared to contemporary music ("History of modern music...")
One representative song of the time- which is characterized by both high popularity and high similarity to the rest of the songs of the 70s- is Dreams by Fleetwood Mac. The 80s

The top attributes of the 80s are very low speechiness, very low liveness, and very high valence. The advent of Music Television (MTV) in the 80s "dramatically changed" music by bolstering the importance of the appearance of an artist ("History of modern music..."). Furthermore, with the advent of Computers, electronic sounds were becoming more common ("History of modern music..."). This is perhaps why we no longer see the high instrumentalness that was a part of the 80s.
One representative song of that time is Everybody Wants to Rule The World by Tears For Fears. You can return by right clicking the page and pressing back if you decide to listen to it here.

The 90s

The top attributes of the 90s are low tempo, very low speechiness, and medium high loudness. Especially compared to the 70s, the 90s hosted a spread of musical genres and styles ("History of modern music..."). Most notably, both highly produced pop songs and rap were popular, with "powerful singers" like Mariah Carey, Celine Dion, and Whitney Houston becoming signature artists of that decade ("History of modern music..."). Note the distributions below. The "split" nature of 90s music can be seen by double peaks in tempo and speechiness, which likely correspond to the two juggernauts of the time, hip-hop and pop.
One representative song of that time is All I Want for Christmas Is You by Mariah Carey. You can return by right clicking the page and pressing back if you decide to listen to it here.

The 2000s and 2010s

The top attributes of the 2000s and 2010s are both very high loudness, high loudness, and very high speechiness. The 2000s introduced genres like Emo, but largely carried on the momentum of the 90s with pop music ("History of modern music..."). Note that like the 90s, the 2000s carries an evenish distribution of speechiness. However, unlike the 90s, the peak in speechiness is on the opposite side of the spectrum - very high. This could signify a transition towards more lyric-based music like that of Eminem who rose to prominence in this period. Interestingly, there was little change between the 2000s and 2010s hence why I grouped them together.
One representative song of the 2000s is How to Save a Life by The Fray. You can return by right clicking the page and pressing back if you decide to listen to it here.

One representative song of the 2010s is Circles by Post Malone. You can return by right clicking the page and pressing back if you decide to listen to it here.

The 2020s

At the time of this project, only about ~1/10 of the 2020s has been completed. But in this short period, the top attributes are very high speechiness, very high danceability, and very high loudness. Like the 2000s and 2010s, there is a shared focus of rap and hip-hop (Sheffield, 2020). Interstingly, there seems to be an increase in focus on highly danceable songs. Perhaps this comes from a resurgence of R&B and the rise of the electronic dance genre, House ("Most Popular...").
